# Manthan · Design Context

## Users
Directors of Revenue Accounting and billing-ops leads at B2B SaaS companies. They open the app at 9am to triage overnight chargebacks, click into one case, scan the agent's brief, and approve or push back. Sessions are short and high-stakes - they don't want personality, they want certainty. They are skeptical of AI; they were burned by demos that looked smart and shipped dumb.

Secondary: the senior people doing the design-partner conversations - ex-Stripe execs, Coral co-founders, finance-leadership advisors. They scan for sloppy patterns the way an editor scans for em-dash overuse.

## Brand Personality
Three words: **precise, restrained, accountable.**

Voice: an investigative-research memo, not a fintech landing page. The agent has done the work and is presenting findings - like a senior analyst writing to a partner, not a chatbot greeting a user. Confidence comes from specifics (real charge IDs, real timestamps, real source links), not from bold colors or motion.

Emotional goal: "an adult is on the other side of this." When the operator sees a verdict, they should trust it the way you trust a one-page memo from someone whose work you already respect.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial precision** - closer to a Wall Street research note or a Times editorial than to a SaaS dashboard. The reference is print: page width, hairline rules, restrained typography, generous whitespace between sections, tabular numerals everywhere a number appears.

- **Theme**: dark, warm-tinted (oklch 0.135 0.006 75 - not pure black). The audience reads this at a desk under office lights or on a laptop at home; warm dark reduces eye strain and signals "not a Discord clone."
- **References**: Bloomberg Terminal restraint, NYT print editorial, Linear's typography density, Ramp's tabular discipline.
- **Anti-references**: Stripe's gradient hero CTAs (signals "marketing surface"), Notion's bubble-pill badges everywhere, MaterialUI cards-in-cards, generic SaaS "look at our beautiful charts" theatre, any AI-generated dashboard that uses cyan-on-dark + purple gradient + neon glow.
- **No glow effects**, no border-stripes, no gradient text, no glassmorphism used as decoration. The only acceptable gradient is the hero iridescence on Landing (already restrained and intentional).

## Design Principles

1. **Hairlines, not borders.** Replace `border-1px-radius-lg` card boxes with full-width sections separated by 1px rules. Cards are reserved for genuinely-grouped content (one case row, one source) - not for every wrapper.

2. **Numbers are typographic.** Every amount, count, ID, timestamp uses `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`. Currency lives in `font-mono` when it's a citation or external_ref, but in `font-display` when it's a headline metric.

3. **Badges are rare.** A status badge appears on a case row OR on its title in detail view - never both. We use color on text, not chips of color around text, whenever it conveys the same info.

4. **Eyebrows replace icons.** Section headers use the uppercase letterspaced "eyebrow" style. Decorative icons above section titles are deleted.

5. **One serif, one sans, one mono.** Display serif: **Spectral** (editorial, contemporary, NOT in the reflex-font monoculture). Body sans: **Geist**. Mono: **Geist Mono**. Instrument Serif is banned because it's in every AI-generated portfolio site in 2025.

6. **Empty states teach.** No "Nothing here" placeholder copy. An empty Inbox tells you why it's empty + what to do next.

7. **Loading states are inline.** A spinning loader replacing the whole content area is jarring. Skeletons or inline `…` for short waits.
